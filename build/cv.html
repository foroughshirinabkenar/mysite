Bio

I received her B.Sc. in Information Technology (IT) from Azarbaijan Shahid Madani University. I also did my master’s degree in IT by focusing on computer and communication networks. I proposed novel algorithms in my research thesis to improve the efficiency of a special kind of optical networks, namely elastic optical networks (EONs), in terms of network delay as well as resource utilization. I received my M.Sc. from Sahand University of Technology, Tabriz, Iran.

I am currently the final semester Ph.D. researcher at The University of Sydney. I joined the Wireless Networking Group (WiNG) in March 2018. I am working under the supervision of Prof. Abbas Jamalipour. My research topic is to provide different quality of service (QoS) requirements such as energy efficiency, delay, and throughput in fog-enabled Internet of Things (IoT) networks. Moreover, I am interested in other related topics, such as the Internet of Vehicles (IoV) and unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs), which can directly affect the performance of Fog-IoT networks.
